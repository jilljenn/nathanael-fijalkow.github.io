The fairy tale

The star-height problem

  • $(a + b^*)^* + a b^*$ has star-height $2$
  • $a^* b^* + (ba)^*$ has star-height $1$

Star-height problem: given a regular language $L$, what is the minimal star-height of a regular expression denoting $L$?

Theorem (Hashiguchi): the star-height problem can be reduced to the boundedness problem of cost automata over finite words
Induces   $f : A^* \to \mathbb{N} \cup \{ \infty \}$ $$f(w) = \text{min} \left\{ \text{max} \text{ counter value in } \rho \mid \rho \text{ accepting run} \right\}$$

The Mostowski index problem

  • $\mu X\ \nu Y\ \phi(X,Y)$ computes greatest, then least fixpoint
  • $\nu X\ \mu Y\ \phi(X,Y)$ computes least, then greatest fixpoint


Mostowski index problem: given a property over graphs, what is the minimal alternation of a modal $\mu$-calculus formula denoting it?


Theorem (Colcombet, Loeding): the Mostowski index problem can be reduced to the boundedness problem of cost automata over infinite trees, which is not known to be decidable

Lesson learned from the past decades:
the two ingredients for dealing with tree automata are
  • determinisation of parity automata over infinite words
  • positional determinacy of parity games

Theorem (Colcombet): cost automata over infinite words can be made history-deterministic

A path out of the forest: finite-memory determinacy for parity games with counters


Eve's dilemma: ensure parity AND keep the counter bounded

The story so far


Theorem (Vanden Boom): Buechi games with counters are finite-memory determined

Theorem (F., Horn, Kuperberg, Skrzypczak):
  • Parity games with counters are finite-memory determined over thin tree graphs
  • Reachability games with counters are NOT finite-memory determined over finite graphs


Theorem (F.): Parity games with counters are finite-memory determined over tree graphs for Eve
