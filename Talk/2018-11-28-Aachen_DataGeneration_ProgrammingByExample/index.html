Data generation

Feynman: What I cannot create, I do not understand.
The learning model should learn the DSL, not the data generation.

Confucius: When a wise man points at the moon, the imbecile examines the finger.

Why?
  • To obtain generalisable models (unseen data)
  • Otherwise the setting does not make sense!

Objectives of data generation

  • Find informative programs and I/O with varied behaviours
  • Beware of the aliasing problem (when two programs agree on I/O)

A closer look at our DSL

The choice of DSL makes data generation hard!
  • Many partial functions: take, access
  • Functions like scanl1(*) and drop are a nightmare

Four data generation methods

Problem: given a program, find interesting I/O

  • Domain restriction (original DeepCoder approach)
  • Non-uniform sampling
  • Constraint-based (leveraging z3)
  • Semantic variation
